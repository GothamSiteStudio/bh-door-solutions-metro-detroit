A patio door is the biggest moving piece of glass in your home, and when it sticks, drafts, or won't lock, you feel it every single day. Whether your old slider has fogged between the panes, jumped its track, or leaks cold air all winter, professional patio door installation fixes it for good. BH Door Solutions installs and replaces sliding, French, and folding glass doors for homes and businesses across metro Detroit.

Sliding Glass Patio Doors

Sliding glass doors are the workhorse of the Michigan home—perfect for decks, patios, and tight spaces where a swinging door won't fit. Our sliding patio door installation covers everything from single-panel replacements to wide multi-panel units, all set on smooth rollers that won't bind or grind. We swap out worn tracks, upgrade to insulated Low-E glass, and seal the frame tight against drafts.

  • Energy-efficient Low-E, argon-filled double-pane glass
  • Heavy-duty rollers and reinforced tracks that glide instead of stick
  • Foot-locks and anti-lift hardware for real security

French Patio Doors

When you want the classic swing-open look and full-width views, French doors deliver. Our french door installation includes both in-swing and out-swing configurations, single and double units, with the option of a fixed panel or a true double-active pair. We hang them plumb and true so both leaves latch cleanly and the astragal seals flush—no gaps, no rattle, no daylight showing through the seam.

  • In-swing or out-swing, single or double-door layouts
  • Multi-point locking hardware for a tight, secure seal
  • Adjustable sills and full weatherstripping for a draft-free close

Multi-Slide & Folding Glass Wall Systems

Want to open an entire wall to the backyard or a restaurant patio? Multi-slide and bi-fold (accordion) glass wall systems stack or pocket away to erase the line between inside and out. These are precision systems—the panels have to be perfectly aligned and the sill drainage has to be right, or they bind and leak. We handle the structural prep, threshold detailing, and panel tuning for both homes and commercial spaces.

  • Multi-slide, pocketing, and bi-fold folding configurations
  • Low-profile, ADA-compliant sill options for commercial jobs
  • Structural opening prep and precise panel alignment

Built for Michigan Winters

Metro Detroit's freeze-thaw cycle is brutal on patio doors. Water works into a worn track or a failed seal, freezes overnight, and pries the door out of alignment until it won't slide or lock. We install units rated for our climate—thermally broken frames, quality weatherstripping, and proper sill drainage—so your door stays tight through January and cool through July.

If your current door is drafty, foggy between the panes, or frozen to its track, those are all signs the seal has already failed and it's time to replace it.

What to Expect: Our Installation Process

Every patio door installation starts with a free, no-obligation in-home estimate, where we measure the opening, check the framing, and give you honest flat-rate pricing up front. On install day, our fully-stocked truck carries the door, hardware, flashing, and sealant we need to finish in one visit.

We remove the old unit, inspect the opening for hidden water or rot damage, set the new door level and square, insulate and flash the frame, then test every lock, latch, and roller before we clean up and go.

What's included

  • Free in-home measurement and a no-obligation written estimate
  • Removal and haul-away of your old door and debris
  • Inspection of the rough opening for hidden water damage or rot
  • Professional leveling, shimming, and squaring of the new unit
  • Insulation, flashing, and weathertight sealing around the frame
  • New locks, handles, rollers, and weatherstripping installed
  • Interior and exterior trim finishing for a clean, finished look
  • A full function test of every lock, latch, and glide before we leave

Patio &amp; Sliding Doors — FAQs

How long does patio door installation take?

Most standard sliding or French patio door installations take a single day, often just a few hours. A straightforward panel replacement goes fast, while a wider multi-slide or folding glass wall system, or an opening that needs framing repair, can take longer. We give you a realistic timeline at your free in-home estimate so there are no surprises on install day.

Should I repair or replace my sliding door?

Replace it if the glass is fogged between the panes, the frame is rotted, or it ices off its track every winter—those point to a failed seal or frame. If the door just sticks or a roller is worn, a repair may be enough. We'll tell you honestly which makes sense at your free estimate, never pushing a replacement you don't need.

Can you install a French door where my old slider is?

Yes, in most cases we can replace a sliding door with French patio doors, or the other way around. The opening width usually works for either style, though we'll check the framing and header first. Swapping styles is a popular upgrade for better curb appeal and airflow. We'll confirm what fits your opening and give you flat-rate pricing at your free estimate.

Do you install patio doors for businesses too?

Yes, we install and replace patio and folding glass wall systems for commercial spaces as well as homes—restaurants, offices, storefronts, and patios. We offer low-profile, ADA-compliant sill options and can schedule around your operations during the business day. One call covers both your residential and commercial door needs across Wayne, Oakland, and Macomb counties.
