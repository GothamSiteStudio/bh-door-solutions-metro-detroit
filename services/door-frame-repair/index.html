The frame and jamb are what hold your door square, sealed, and locked, so when they split, rot, or get kicked in, the whole opening stops doing its job. If you need door frame repair in Metro Detroit, BH Door Solutions rebuilds the frame and jamb so the door closes tight, latches clean, and keeps Michigan weather where it belongs, outside. Split, Rotted & Warped Frames and Jambs

Wood frames and jambs take a beating in Michigan. Years of freeze-thaw, wind-driven rain, and snowmelt wicking up from the threshold cause the bottom of a jamb to soften, split, and rot, often hidden behind the weatherstripping and trim where you can't see it until the door starts sagging or the deadbolt won't line up.

Not every soft spot means a full teardown. Where the damage is limited we cut out the rotted section, splice in new solid or composite jamb stock, and prime and seal it so water can't get back in. When the split runs the length of the jamb or the frame has warped out of square, a full door frame repair or replacement is the honest call, and we'll show you which one you're actually looking at.

  • Soft, spongy, or crumbling wood at the base of the jamb
  • Cracks or splits running down the strike or hinge side
  • A frame bowed out of square so the door won't sit flush
  • Peeling paint, dark staining, or a musty smell near the threshold

Frame & Jamb Replacement

When a frame is too far gone to patch, replacing it is the fix that lasts. We remove the old frame and jamb, check the rough opening and subfloor for hidden water damage, and set a new frame plumb, level, and square, the three things that decide whether a door swings and latches right for years.

We match your existing setup, from standard residential prehung openings to heavier commercial steel frames on high-traffic entrances. Every replacement gets properly shimmed, fastened into the framing studs, insulated at the gaps, and sealed, so you're not just getting a new frame, you're getting a door that opens easy, closes tight, and locks securely.

Kick-In & Break-In Jamb Repair

A forced entry almost never breaks the lock, it splinters the jamb where the strike plate is screwed in, which makes kick-in damage one of the more urgent kinds of door frame repair we handle. After a break-in you're often left with a working deadbolt but a shattered jamb that won't hold it, so we treat these calls as a priority and get out same-day when possible during business hours to secure the opening.

We replace the split jamb section, reinforce the strike area with a heavy-duty strike plate and long screws that anchor into the wall framing instead of just the trim, and re-hang the door so it closes and locks like new. Where you want extra peace of mind, we can add a reinforced strike box or security jamb hardware so the next attempt has a lot more to get through.

  • Splintered jamb around the strike plate or deadbolt
  • Door that latches but no longer feels secure
  • Reinforced strike plates and longer framing screws
  • Optional security jamb hardware for added protection

Weatherstripping & Draft Sealing

If you feel a draft or see daylight around a closed door, the seal between the door and the frame has failed, and in a Michigan winter that gap is pouring heat straight out of your home. Weatherstripping & draft sealing is the quiet part of frame work that makes the biggest difference to your comfort and your energy bills.

We replace worn or compressed weatherstripping in the jamb, adjust the door so it meets the seal evenly all the way around, and close up the gaps that let in cold air, water, and street noise. The result is a door that shuts with a solid, quiet thud instead of a rattle, and a home that holds its heat where you paid to put it.

Thresholds, Sweeps & Weatherization

The threshold and door sweep are the seal along the bottom of the opening, and they're the first parts to wear out because they take every footstep, every snow boot, and every puddle. A worn sweep or a warped, pitted threshold lets cold air, water, and even pests slip in under the door, and it's a common hidden cause of a rotting jamb.

We replace door sweeps, reset or swap adjustable thresholds, and re-establish a clean seal at the sill so water drains away from the frame instead of into it. Weatherization is the part of door frame repair that keeps the frame dry, so we seal the whole opening, top, sides, and bottom, as one system that lasts.

  • Worn, torn, or dragging door sweeps replaced
  • Adjustable thresholds reset for a tight bottom seal
  • Sill sealed so water drains away from the frame
  • Whole-opening weatherization, top to bottom

Door Frame &amp; Jamb Repair — FAQs

Can you repair just the frame, or do I have to replace the whole door?

In most cases we can repair the frame and jamb without touching the door itself. If the damage is limited rot, a split, or a kicked-in strike area, we splice in new jamb stock and reinforce it, saving you the cost of a full replacement. We only recommend replacing the frame when it's warped badly out of square or the rot has spread too far, and we'll show you which you're facing.

My door frame is rotting at the bottom. What causes that in Michigan?

Bottom-of-jamb rot is caused by water, and Michigan gives it plenty of chances. Snowmelt, wind-driven rain, and freeze-thaw let moisture wick up into the wood behind the weatherstripping and threshold, where it sits and slowly softens the jamb. We cut out the rot, splice in sealed replacement stock, and correct the drainage and seal so it doesn't creep back.

Someone kicked in my door. Can you repair the jamb?

Yes, jamb repair after a break-in is one of the most common repairs we do. A forced entry usually splinters the jamb around the strike plate rather than breaking the lock, so we replace the damaged section and reinforce the strike area with heavy-duty plates and long screws anchored into the framing. We treat these as a priority and get out same-day when possible during business hours.

Will fixing the frame stop the drafts around my door?

Yes, sealing the frame is exactly how we stop door drafts. Most cold air sneaks in through failed weatherstripping in the jamb, a worn sweep, or a gap at the threshold, not through the door slab itself. Our weatherstripping and draft sealing resets that seal all the way around the opening, so the door shuts tight and your heat stays inside where it belongs.
