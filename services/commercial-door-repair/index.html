Storefront, Aluminum & Glass Door Repair

Aluminum storefront systems and frameless herculite glass doors take a beating: constant traffic, seasonal expansion, and Michigan's freeze-thaw cycles that heave entry slabs and throw doors out of square. When a storefront door drags, won't self-close, or the pivot sags, we realign the frame, rebuild or replace pivots and pin hinges, and get the sweep sealing against the threshold again.

We also handle storefront & glass door repair for cracked, fogged, or shattered lites, with tempered safety glass and insulated units cut to spec, plus commercial entry doors in aluminum, wood, and steel. If a break-in or storm left your front damaged, we board it secure and replace the glass and hardware promptly, same-day when the schedule allows during business hours.

  • Aluminum storefront & herculite glass door realignment
  • Tempered and insulated glass replacement cut to spec
  • Pivot, pin-hinge, and threshold repair
  • Commercial entry doors in aluminum, steel, and wood

Panic Bars, Exit Devices & Commercial Hardware

Panic bars and exit devices aren't optional. Fire and building codes require them on most commercial egress doors, and when one sticks, dogs open when it shouldn't, or won't latch, you have both a security gap and a compliance problem. We repair, adjust, and replace rim, mortise, and vertical-rod panic bars & exit devices, including electrified hardware tied to access control.

We also service the hardware that makes a commercial door work every day: door closers that drift or slam, often from cold-thickened fluid in winter; mortise and cylindrical locksets; continuous and heavy-duty hinges; and weatherstripping that's let the drafts in. Dialing in the closer sweep and latch timing keeps the door protecting itself for years.

  • Panic bars & exit devices: rim, mortise, vertical rod
  • Electrified and access-control-integrated hardware
  • Commercial door closers adjusted or replaced
  • Locksets, hinges, thresholds, and weatherstripping

Hollow-Metal, Man Doors & Fire-Rated Doors

Hollow-metal doors and frames, the steel man doors on the sides and backs of nearly every commercial building, rust at the bottom, rack out of square, and bind when frames shift or hinges wear. Michigan road salt and moisture only speed that up. We repair and replace hollow-metal doors, reinforce frames, and swap corroded thresholds so your service entrance opens, closes, and locks the way it should.

Fire-rated doors carry a label for a reason, and any repair has to keep that rating intact. We service fire-rated openings, covering self-closing hardware, positive latching, correct gap tolerances, and intact labels, so your building stays code-compliant and ready to pass inspection.

  • Hollow-metal (man) door and frame repair
  • Rusted threshold and corroded frame replacement
  • Fire-rated door service that preserves the label
  • Self-closing and positive-latching adjustments

Automatic Operators & ADA Accessibility

Automatic swing and slide operators and low-energy ADA push-button operators keep your entrance accessible and welcoming, until a sensor fails, the door reverses on its own, or the operator won't hold open. We troubleshoot and repair automatic operators, replace sensors, actuators, and safety beams, and install new ADA-compliant operators so every customer, including those using wheelchairs and walkers, can get through your door without a fight.

We also verify the details that keep you compliant: opening force, hold-open time, and reversing sensors all set to accessibility requirements, so your entrance works for everyone and passes inspection.

  • Automatic swing & slide operator repair
  • ADA push-button (low-energy) operator installation
  • Sensor, actuator, and safety-beam replacement
  • Opening force and hold-open time set to ADA spec

Commercial Doors — FAQs

Do you repair storefront and glass doors, or only replace them?

We do both, and repair is usually the first option. Most storefront and glass door problems, like misaligned frames, worn pivots, failed closers, or cracked glass, can be fixed without replacing the whole door. We assess the frame and hardware, replace only what's failed, and give you an honest flat-rate quote. Full replacement is recommended only when a repair won't hold.

Can you fix a commercial door that won't lock or close?

Yes. Doors that won't lock, latch, or close are among the most common commercial repairs we handle, and we treat them promptly, same-day when the schedule allows during business hours. The cause is usually a worn closer, a misaligned strike, a failed panic device, or a frame knocked out of square. We diagnose it on-site and secure your opening the same visit whenever possible.

Do you service panic bars and fire-rated doors to code?

Yes. We repair and replace panic bars and exit devices and service fire-rated openings so they stay code-compliant and inspection-ready. That means proper positive latching, self-closing hardware, correct gap tolerances, and intact fire labels. If an inspector flagged your egress or fire doors, we can bring the hardware and clearances back into compliance.

How much does commercial door repair cost?

It depends on the door, the hardware, and what's failed. A closer adjustment costs far less than a full storefront pivot rebuild or glass replacement. Typical commercial door repair ranges widely based on parts and labor, so we give a free, no-obligation estimate with flat-rate pricing before any work starts. Your exact price depends on your door, so call us for a quick quote.
