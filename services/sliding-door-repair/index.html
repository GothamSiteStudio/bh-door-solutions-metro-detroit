Sliding Door Problems We Fix

Most sliding door trouble starts small—a little drag, a click that won't catch—and gets worse until the door barely moves. Our sliding glass door repair covers the full range of issues on residential patio doors and commercial storefront sliders alike, usually in a single visit with parts already on the truck.

Whatever your door is doing, we've fixed it before. Here's what we most often see and repair:

  • Worn or broken rollers that make the door drag, grind, and stick
  • Bent, dented, or dirty tracks that throw the door off its path
  • Off-track doors that have jumped the rail and won't slide at all
  • Sliders that won't lock, latch, or close flush against the frame
  • Foggy, cracked, or shattered glass panels

Roller & Track Repair

The rollers are the small wheels at the bottom of your door that carry all its weight, and they take a beating. Grit works into the wheels, the bearings wear out, and the door starts to grind against the frame. We swap worn roller assemblies for heavy-duty replacements and adjust their height so the door hangs level and rolls smooth.

The track matters just as much. A track that's bent, packed with debris, or corroded will chew through brand-new rollers in months. Our roller & track repair includes straightening the track, clearing it out, and lubricating it—and if a door has jumped off the rail entirely, we re-seat it and correct whatever knocked it off in the first place.

Sliding Door Glass Replacement

When you see fog, haze, or moisture trapped between the panes, the sealed glass unit has failed. That's more than an eyesore—it means the insulating gas is gone and you're losing heat straight through the door. We handle sliding door glass replacement by swapping the failed insulated glass unit while keeping your existing frame, which restores clarity and stops the energy loss.

Cracked or shattered panels—from a stray ball, a storm, or an attempted break-in—get replaced the same way. We measure, source the right glass for your specific door, and install it so the panel is clear, solid, and sealed tight again.

Sliders That Won't Lock or Close

A sliding door that won't lock is both a security worry and a draft problem. Usually the culprit is a worn latch, a misaligned strike, or a door sitting too low on failed rollers to line up with the lock. We repair or replace the lock hardware and adjust the door until it closes flush and latches with an easy, solid catch.

If your door was forced or damaged in a break-in attempt or a storm, this is exactly the kind of urgent repair we tackle promptly—often same-day when the schedule allows during business hours—so your home is buttoned up and secure again.

Michigan-Ready Repairs & What to Expect

Michigan's freeze-thaw cycle is brutal on sliding doors. Water gets into the track, freezes, and warps the aluminum; cold turns worn weatherstripping brittle; and a slider that won't seal lets winter drafts pour in. We fix the mechanical problem and reseal the door so it holds up through the next season—not just until the next cold snap.

Sliding Door Repair Across Metro Detroit's Suburbs

Sliding doors age differently depending on where you live and when your home was built, and we work on the full range across Metro Detroit every week. In the postwar brick ranches of Warren and St. Clair Shores, we often find original aluminum-frame sliders whose shallow tracks have worn thin and whose small steel rollers were never built for decades of daily use. The colonials and split-levels that filled Troy, Rochester Hills, and Farmington Hills from the 1970s onward tend to carry heavier wood and clad-wood patio doors, where humid Michigan summers swell the stiles and the sill until the panel drags. Newer subdivisions in Novi and Canton mostly run vinyl sliders, which hold their shape well but ride on plastic tandem rollers that flatten and crack as they age.

The track type matters as much as the door. Worn aluminum tracks can often be revived with a stainless track cap instead of a full track replacement, which saves tearing out the frame. Bent or dented tracks get straightened and cleaned, and corroded rollers are swapped for sealed, height-adjustable assemblies matched to your door's weight, whether that's a light single-pane slider or a heavy insulated glass panel.

We also handle the details that make a slider livable: sticky screen doors that derail in the wind, handles and mortise-style latches that seize up in the cold, and weatherstripping that lets a winter draft pour through the meeting rail. For businesses, we service heavier commercial sliders and multi-panel systems with the same stocked-truck, one-visit approach.

Glass is its own story. Close to the water in St. Clair Shores and Grosse Pointe Woods, constant lake humidity is tough on sealed units, and fogged or hazy panels are one of the most common calls we get there. We replace the insulated glass unit while keeping your existing frame, which restores the view and the insulation without the price of a new door. The same goes for panels cracked by a storm or a stray baseball anywhere from Royal Oak to Ferndale, including the narrower sliders common in condos and townhouses there.

Wherever you are within about 35 miles of Detroit, across Wayne, Oakland, and Macomb counties, the visit works the same way: we diagnose the rollers, track, glass, and lock together, quote you a flat rate up front, and finish most sliding door repairs in a single trip because the parts are already on the truck. Same-day service is available across the suburbs when the schedule allows, so call early if your slider has jumped the track or won't lock tonight.

Sliding Door Repair — FAQs

Can you repair my sliding door, or do I need a whole new one?

In most cases we can repair it, not replace it. Worn rollers, bent tracks, failed locks, and off-track panels are all fixable with parts we stock on our trucks. A full door replacement is usually only necessary when the frame itself is damaged or rotted. We'll tell you honestly during your free estimate.

Why is my sliding glass door so hard to open?

A slider that drags or sticks almost always comes down to worn rollers or a dirty, bent track. Dirt, grit, and Michigan freeze-thaw grind down the roller wheels and dent the aluminum track over time. We replace the rollers, straighten and clean the track, and adjust the height so it glides freely again.

Can you replace just the foggy glass panel?

Yes. When you see fog or moisture between the panes, the glass seal has failed—but the frame is usually still fine. We replace the insulated glass unit itself rather than the whole door, which restores clarity and stops the energy loss. Cracked or shattered panels get swapped the same way.

How much does sliding glass door repair cost?

Most sliding door repairs are affordable and far cheaper than replacing the whole door. Typical roller and track repairs run in the low-to-mid hundreds, while glass panel replacement depends on size and pane type. Your exact price depends on the parts and door, so we give you a free, no-obligation estimate upfront with flat-rate pricing.
