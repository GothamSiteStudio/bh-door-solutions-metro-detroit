Pre-Hung & Slab Doors

Every interior door installation starts with picking the right type for the opening. A prehung door arrives already mounted in its own frame with the hinges attached — the cleanest option when the old jamb is damaged, out of square, or you're changing the door size. A slab is just the door itself, which we hang on your existing hinges and frame when the jamb is still solid. Prehung door installation takes longer but gives you a fresh, square opening; slab swaps are faster and easier on the budget.

Michigan's humidity swings are hard on interior doors. Wood absorbs summer moisture and swells, then shrinks in the dry winter heat — which is why a door that closed fine in July binds by January. We set proper reveal gaps, shim the jambs true, and hang the hinges so your door keeps swinging clean through every freeze-thaw season.

  • Prehung installs when the frame is damaged, out of square, or being resized
  • Slab swaps when your existing jamb is solid and true
  • Correct reveal gaps set for Michigan's seasonal swell and shrink

Closet & Bifold Doors

Closet and bifold doors take more abuse than almost any door in the house — kids yank them, tracks bend, and cheap rollers jump the rail. We install bifold, sliding bypass, and hinged closet doors on tracks that actually stay aligned, with the pivots and guides adjusted so panels fold flat and glide without catching. Whether you're finishing a bedroom, a hall closet, or the laundry, we make sure the doors clear the carpet, the trim, and each other.

Sliding Barn Doors

Sliding barn doors are the upgrade everyone wants — but done wrong, they wobble, scrape the wall, or don't fully cover the opening. Barn door installation is really about the hardware and the backing: we locate the studs or add solid blocking behind the drywall so the track carries the door's full weight without sagging. Then we hang the door with the right gap, add a floor guide to stop the sway, and set soft-close stops so it lands quiet.

The result is the farmhouse look that holds up, not a decorative piece that rattles every time you slide it. We handle single doors and double-door openings, on wood or on rooms where you want the extra light of a glass panel.

Pocket Doors

Pocket doors are the space-saver for tight bathrooms, closets, and hallways where a swinging door would waste floor space. We install new pocket-door kits and repair existing ones — replacing bent tracks, worn rollers, and split guides so the door slides fully into the wall and back out without jumping the rail. When a pocket door disappears crooked or won't stay open, it's almost always a track or roller problem, and our stocked trucks carry the parts to fix it in one visit during business hours.

Interior French Doors

Interior French doors bring light and a wider, open feel between rooms — living room to office, or a primary bedroom to a sitting area. The trick with a French pair is alignment: both doors have to meet evenly in the middle, latch clean, and swing without one dragging. We set the jamb dead plumb, adjust the hinges, and dial in the astragal and strike so the pair closes flush every time.

Solid or glass-panel, we hang them so the center seam stays tight and both doors work as one — a finished interior door installation you'll notice every time you walk through.

Interior Doors — FAQs

What's the difference between a prehung door and a slab door?

A prehung door comes already mounted in a complete frame with the hinges attached, while a slab is just the door panel you hang on an existing frame. Prehung is best when your jamb is damaged, out of square, or you're resizing the opening. Slab installs are faster and cost less when the existing frame is still solid and square.

How long does interior door installation take?

Most single interior door installations take one to two hours, including removing the old door, hanging the new one, and adjusting the hardware. A slab swap on a good frame is quicker; a prehung door, barn door, or a whole house of doors takes longer. When our schedule allows, we can often finish same-day installs during business hours.

Can you install a sliding barn door on any wall?

In most cases, yes, but the wall needs solid backing to carry the door's weight. During barn door installation we locate the studs or add blocking behind the drywall so the track won't sag or pull loose over time. We also confirm the wall beside the opening is wide enough for the door to slide fully clear.

Do you repair pocket doors, or only install new ones?

We do both. We install new pocket-door kits and repair existing ones that stick, jump the track, or won't stay open — usually a bent track, worn rollers, or a broken guide. Because our trucks are stocked with common pocket-door parts, we can often diagnose and fix the problem in a single visit during business hours.
