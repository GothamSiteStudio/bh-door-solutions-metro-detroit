Door installation & repair St. Clair Shores homeowners rely on

St. Clair Shores is a water town, and the homes show it. Along the finger canals off the Nautical Mile, houses open right onto the water with sliding patio doors and rear entries that face the wind, spray, and sun coming off Lake St. Clair. Those doors take a beating an inland home never sees — tracks pack with grit, rollers wear out, and glass seals fail after enough seasons. We install and repair patio and sliding doors built to stay weather-tight where the lake sits a few feet from your back step.

Move a few blocks inland and the streets off Harper and Greater Mack fill with the well-kept 1950s ranches St. Clair Shores is known for. Many still wear their original entry doors and frames, and after decades of Michigan freeze-thaw the jambs shift, the weatherstripping crumbles, and a February draft finds its way in. Swapping a tired entry door for a properly sealed, insulated one is one of the fastest ways to cut the draft and lift the curb appeal on these classic homes.

Michigan weather here doesn't ease up. Summer humidity off the lake swells wood doors until they stick; winter cold shrinks and warps them until they won't latch; and the storms that roll across Macomb County can leave a door that won't close or lock right. Whether it's a swollen door on a Ten Mile ranch or storm damage on a canal-front home, we tackle it promptly — same-day when the schedule allows during our business hours.

Door replacement in St. Clair Shores, MI

When St. Clair Shores homeowners ask whether it's time to replace a door, we look at the frame, the seal, and the hardware — not just the slab. If replacement wins, you get an exact flat-rate quote covering the door, labor, and cleanup. We replace everything from patio & sliding doors to entry & front doors across Macomb County.

The doors St. Clair Shores homes need most

For St. Clair Shores, patio and sliding door work tops the list — the canal and lakeside homes lean hard on glass doors that face the water, so track repair, roller and glass replacement, and full patio-door installation come up constantly. Right behind it is entry door replacement for the area's 1950s ranches, where an insulated, well-sealed front door pays off against winter drafts. We also handle storm doors, interior doors, and commercial doors for the shops and offices along Harper and Jefferson.
