Door installation & repair Troy homeowners rely on

Troy grew up around big-lot subdivisions and executive colonials — the established neighborhoods off Wattles and Long Lake roads, and the newer luxury builds toward Square Lake. On homes like these, the front door is the whole first impression, which is why so many Troy owners come to us for custom and premium entry doors: fiberglass and solid-wood slabs, sidelights, transoms, and wide double-door entries that suit a colonial or a modern build without looking builder-grade.

Michigan's freeze-thaw cycle is hard on Troy doors. A wood slab that swells shut in a humid August and then rattles with a January draft usually isn't the door's fault — it's a worn threshold, a failed sweep, or hinges that have shifted with the seasons. We see the same thing on the patio and slider doors off the back of Troy's ranches and colonials, where iced-over tracks and fogged, seal-failed glass turn up every spring.

Troy isn't only homes. The Big Beaver corridor and the office parks along Rochester and Crooks roads mean we also handle commercial storefront and service doors — glass entrances that won't latch, closers that slam, and steel back doors that need to lock every night. Whether it's a colonial off Livernois or a suite near the Somerset Collection, you get the same local crew and the same upfront pricing.

Door replacement in Troy, MI

When Troy homeowners ask whether it's time to replace a door, we look at the frame, the seal, and the hardware — not just the slab. If replacement wins, you get an exact flat-rate quote covering the door, labor, and cleanup. We replace everything from entry & front doors to patio & sliding doors across Oakland County.

The doors Troy homes need most

For Troy specifically, entry-door installation and replacement is where we spend most of our time — high-end fiberglass and wood front doors, double-door entries, and matching storm doors that protect the investment from Michigan weather. Right behind that is repair work: sagging or misaligned doors, failed weatherstripping and drafts, sliders that won't glide, and locks or handlesets that quit working. Because so many Troy homes carry real value, owners here want the job done once, done right, and done to match the house — which is exactly how we work.
