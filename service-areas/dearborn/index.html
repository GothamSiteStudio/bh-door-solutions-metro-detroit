Door installation & repair Dearborn homeowners rely on

Dearborn's housing is older than most of metro Detroit's, and it shows at the front door. The brick bungalows, Tudors, and center-entrance colonials built before 1960—many raised for Ford workers—still wear their original wood and steel entry and storm doors. Decades of Michigan freeze-thaw winters swell, warp, and rust those doors until they stick, sag on their hinges, or let the cold pour straight in.

From the tightly packed streets of East Dearborn around Warren Avenue to the West Dearborn homes near Michigan Avenue, the Ford Homes Historic District, and the stately properties by the Henry Ford estate at Fair Lane, we see the same story: hardworking doors that have earned a replacement. The freeze-thaw swings along the Rouge River corridor are hard on hinges, sills, and weatherstripping, and a drafty door drives up your heating bill in a hurry once winter sets in.

We also handle the storefronts and shops along Michigan Avenue and Warren Avenue, plus offices near Ford world headquarters. Whether it's a decades-old front door on an Aviation Sub bungalow or a commercial entrance that won't latch, we match new doors to the character of Dearborn homes and businesses while sealing out the Michigan weather for good.
