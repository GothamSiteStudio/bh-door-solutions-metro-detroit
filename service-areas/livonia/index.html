Door installation & repair Livonia homeowners rely on

Livonia is a city of postwar brick ranches and split-levels, and most of them still wear the doors they were built with. From Rosedale Gardens and Coventry Gardens to the tri-levels around Kimberly Oaks and Bicentennial Estates, these 1950s-through-'70s homes were framed decades ago—and after that many freeze-thaw winters, original entry and side doors rarely close as square as they once did. We match new doors to the scale and style of your home, whether it's a low-slung ranch off Six Mile or a quad-level near Newburgh.

The big attached garages and backyard patios that define Livonia streets mean two doors take a beating here: the sliding patio door off the family room and the service door between the garage and the house. Decades of use leave patio rollers dragging and glass seals fogged, while garage-side doors warp and let cold air pour into living space. Whether you're near Laurel Park Place, off Farmington Road, or tucked into the neighborhoods along Merriman and Middlebelt, these are the replacements we handle week in and week out.

Michigan's freeze-thaw swing is hard on Livonia doors. Winter cold shrinks weatherstripping and lets drafts whistle past thresholds, spring thaw shifts porch slabs and door frames out of square, and humid summers swell wood until doors stick and stall. A door that won't latch or seal isn't just annoying—it's heat leaking straight out of your home. We fix the seal, the swing, and the security in one visit so your door works through every Michigan season.

The doors Livonia homes need most

For Livonia's owner-occupied ranches and split-levels, the work that matters most is straightforward: replacing tired entry doors that no longer seal, swapping failed sliding patio doors for smooth-rolling, energy-efficient units, and installing solid garage-side service doors that keep the cold out. Because so many Livonia homes still carry their factory-era doors, weather-tight replacement and installation—not just quick patches—deliver the biggest gain in comfort, security, and curb appeal.
