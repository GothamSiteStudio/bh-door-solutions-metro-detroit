Door installation & repair Pontiac homeowners rely on

Renovation is the story in Pontiac right now, from the blocks around the Woodward Loop downtown to the residential streets fanning out from it. Landmarks like the M1 Concourse nearby have brought fresh energy, and houses that sat tired for decades are getting new life. Old doors are usually on the punch list: slabs planed thin by past fixes, jambs out of square, hinges buried under a dozen coats of paint.

Our approach in older houses is repair-first. A hundred-year-old door is often better material than anything at a big-box store, and realigning it, re-mortising hinges, and rebuilding the strike side of a frame can give it decades more service. When a frame is rotted or a previous owner cut too many corners, we rebuild the jamb square and hang a new insulated door that respects the house's proportions.

Security and weather matter too: fresh deadbolts and reinforced strikes on solid doors, storm doors over vintage entries, and new weatherstripping to close the winter gaps these houses are known for. See the full list of communities we serve on our service areas page.

Door replacement in Pontiac, MI

Thinking about door replacement in Pontiac? We'll walk you through material choices, energy efficiency, and real pricing before you commit, and if a repair will honestly do the job, we'll say so. Most Pontiac replacements, including entry doors and storm doors, are finished the same day we start.

Frame and jamb repair for settled houses

A door only works as well as its frame. Our door frame and jamb repair rebuilds split, rotted, and out-of-square frames so the door closes on the first push, and our general door repair service handles sagging hinges, sticking slabs, and doors that no longer latch.

Locks and hardware that actually turn

Original mortise locks and worn knobs are charming until one seizes on a cold morning. We install and repair locks and hardware, from new deadbolts and handlesets to freeing a lock that sticks every winter, matched to the age and style of the house.

The doors Pontiac homes need most

Pontiac's list is repair-heavy: frame and jamb rebuilds, realignment of settled doors, hardware and lock work, and weatherstripping to cut drafts. Entry door replacement follows, where a rotted or badly altered original is holding a renovation back. We give free estimates, tell you honestly what is worth saving, and stand behind the work with our guarantee.

Door service in Pontiac — FAQs

Do you serve Pontiac and its historic neighborhoods?

Yes, we serve all of Pontiac, from the historic neighborhoods around the Woodward Loop downtown to the residential streets across the city. We work throughout central Oakland County, and we are glad to confirm your block when you call.

How quickly can you come out for a door repair in Pontiac?

Often the same day or the next business day, whenever our schedule allows. For a door that will not lock, close, or latch, call early in the day during our business hours, Sunday through Thursday 9am to 5pm and Friday 9am to noon, and we will give you an honest arrival window.

Can you repair the original doors in an older Pontiac house?

Whenever it makes sense, yes. Older doors are often better material than modern replacements, so we realign, re-mortise hinges, rebuild frames, and update hardware first. If a door or jamb is truly gone, we will show you why and quote an insulated replacement that suits the house.
