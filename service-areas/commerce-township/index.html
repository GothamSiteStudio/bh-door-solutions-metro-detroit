Door installation & repair Commerce Township homeowners rely on

Lake homes lean on their glass. Patio and sliding doors face the water, the deck, and the weather that comes across both, so sun and moisture work on seals and rollers faster than they do a few miles inland. A slider that drags or an entry that sweats in winter is usually not a mystery: the parts that fight the weather are simply worn out.

We repair sliders with new rollers, cleaned tracks, and fresh weatherstripping, and when a unit is past saving we install patio doors built for Michigan conditions. The newer subdivisions off M-5 and Union Lake Road bring the other half of the work: builder-grade entries and interior doors aging on schedule, replaced with units measured to the actual opening.

From the streets wrapped around Commerce and Lower Straits lakes to the neighborhoods near Proud Lake, we cover the whole township. The full list of communities we serve is on our service areas page.

Door service in Commerce Township — FAQs

Do you serve all of Commerce Township?

Yes. We cover the whole township, from the lakefront streets around Commerce and Lower Straits lakes to the newer neighborhoods off the M-5 connector. We are already working across Oakland County most days, so scheduling a visit is usually quick.

How quickly can you come out for a door repair in Commerce Township?

Often the same day or the next business day, whenever our schedule allows. For a door that will not lock, close, or latch, call early in the day during our business hours, Sunday through Thursday 9am to 5pm and Friday 9am to noon, and we will give you an honest arrival window.

Can you fix a lake-facing slider instead of replacing it?

Usually, yes. Most tired sliders need new rollers, track repair, and fresh weatherstripping rather than a whole new unit, and we carry common parts on the truck. If the frame or glass is beyond saving, we will quote a replacement honestly.
